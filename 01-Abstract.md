Abstract

Pet service organizations commonly face scattered information, fragmented workflows, and difficulties in unified tracking of appointments and orders across adoption, boarding, training, merchandise sales, and health records, which leads to unstable user experience, delayed management statistics, and insufficient service coordination efficiency. This study targets the pet home business scenario and designs a front-end/back-end separated information management system: the front end uses Vue 3 and Element Plus to build portal and management interfaces, while the back end is built on Spring Boot 3.2, MyBatis-Plus, Spring Security, JWT, and MySQL to provide a unified service layer covering users and permissions, pet information and health records, adoption and boarding, service and training appointments, products and orders, announcements, and statistics. At the business layer, the system forms a closed loop from information publishing and appointment requests to order processing and review feedback, and it enforces consistent workflows through unified data dictionaries and status transition rules. Role- and menu-based access control supports multi-role collaboration and operational boundaries, while the announcement and content modules strengthen operational support. Based on these design decisions, the system improves traceability for order fulfillment, scheduling, and health records, and provides stable data support for subsequent operational analysis and service quality control.

Keywords: pet home; adoption and boarding; service appointments; Spring Boot
